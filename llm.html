Large Language Models (LLMs) - Basics

  • Definition: AI models trained on vast amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like language.
  • Examples: GPT-4, BERT, LLaMA, PaLM.
  • Key Concepts:
    • Tokenization: Breaking text into smaller units (words, subwords).
    • Embeddings: Converting words into numerical representations.
    • Transformers: Neural network architecture enabling deep contextual understanding.
    • Fine-tuning: Adapting a pre-trained model to specific tasks.
  • Applications:
    • Chatbots & Virtual Assistants
    • Content Generation (Text, Code, etc.)
    • Machine Translation
    • Text Summarization
    • Sentiment Analysis
  • Challenges:
    • Bias & Ethical Concerns
    • Hallucinations (Generating incorrect information)
    • Computational Costs
    • Data Privacy Issues
